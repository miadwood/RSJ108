How to become a news anchor

Assembly time: 4+ years
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you wondering how the anchors on news channels got their job? Follow the steps and you'll be on TV in no time!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A Bachelor's degree in Broadcast Journalism
  • Internship experience in broadcasting
  • Must be an early bird
  • A good smile
  • A good attitude
  • Must look presentable

  1. Major in Broadcast Journalism in College

    This is necessary to learn about the journalism and broadcasting industry before being on air to give news to the public.

  2. Get experience in college.

    Remember, internships and other expeerience are key and you are much more likely to be hired as an anchor.

    Photo of Briana Collins

  3. Be available any time of day, even in the early hours of 2 AM!

    Say goodbye to sleeping in! If you make it far enough to be hired, you need to be up and ready hours before the cameras even start rolling.

  4. Always have a good attitude.

    No one wants to watch a frowing monotone anchor on the news. Have a potitive outlook and good character even when talking about negative things.

  5. Look presentable!

    News anchors always look professional. The outfits are always modest and crazy hair colors, visible tattoos, and outragous piercings are usually unacceptable.
